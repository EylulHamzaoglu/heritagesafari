Museums in Turkey

Turkey's museums showcase its rich historical and cultural heritage, with origins dating back to the Ottoman Empire and the establishment of the Republic. The first museum, "Müze-i Hümayun" (Imperial Museum), was established in 1846 in Istanbul's Hagia Irene Church. Museums across Turkey display artifacts from archaeological excavations and cultural objects from various periods. Notable museums include Topkapi Palace, Hagia Sophia, Antalya Museum, and the Museum of Anatolian Civilizations in Ankara, attracting millions of visitors annually.

Exploring the Treasures of France's Museums

Turkey's museums house important artifacts reflecting its rich historical and cultural heritage. Discover the splendor of the Ottoman Empire at Topkapı Sarayı and a wide range of artifacts from the Hittites to the Roman Empire at the Anadolu Medeniyetleri Müzesi. At the Zeugma Mozaik Müzesi, you can see the artistic and cultural richness of ancient mosaics.
